About Me

Biography

I was born in 1998 in a city called Murcia, in the south-east of Spain. After my passion towards studying the university grew during my adolescence, I started the physics degree in the University of Murcia in 2016. After 2 years I transfered to the Complutense University of Madrid (UCM) to specialize in fundamental physics and astrophysics in the last years of the degree. After that, I completed my Masters in Astrophysics also at UCM. During this time I did a 6 month internship at ESAC/ESA in science communication.

Currently I am finishing my 2nd year of PhD at the Institute of Astrophysics and Space Sciences (IA) and University of Porto (UP), under the supervision of Dr. Nuno C. Santos and Dr. Vardan Zh. Adibekyan. My thesis, linked to the Paranal solar ESPRESSO Telescope (PoET), focuses on doing a radial velocity line-by-line analysis of disk-resolved observations of the Sun in order to achive the precision needed to uncover Earth-like planets around Sun-like stars.
